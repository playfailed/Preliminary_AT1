Factorising

To solve quadratic equations through factorization, follow these steps:

  1. Identify the Quadratic Equation:
    A quadratic equation is in the form (ax^2 + bx + c = 0),
  2. Factor the Equation:
    Look for two numbers that multiply to (ac) and add to (b).
  3. Set Each Factor Equal to Zero:
    Once you have the factors, set each factor equal to zero and solve for (x).
    This gives you the solutions to the quadratic equation.
  4. Verify the Solutions:
    Substitute each solution back into the original equation to verify that it is indeed a solution.

Solve the quadratic equation - 7 + 12 = 0). Step 1: Identify the Quadratic Equation The given equation is (x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0). Step 2: Factor the Equation To factor the equation, we look for two numbers that multiply to (12) (the constant term) and add to (-7) (the coefficient of (x)). The numbers that satisfy these conditions are (-2) and (-6). So, the equation can be factored as: [x^2 - 7x + 12 = (x - 2)(x - 6)] Step 3: Set Each Factor Equal to Zero Now, we set each factor equal to zero and solve for (x): For (x - 2 = 0), we find (x = 2). For (x - 6 = 0), we find (x = 6). Step 4: Verify the Solutions To verify these solutions, we substitute (x = 2) and (x = 6) back into the original equation: For (x = 2), (2^2 - 7(2) + 12 = 4 - 14 + 12 = 0), which confirms (x = 2) is a solution. For (x = 6), (6^2 - 7(6) + 12 = 36 - 42 + 12 = 0), which confirms (x = 6) is a solution. Conclusion The solutions to the quadratic equation (x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0) are (x = 2) and (x = 6).
